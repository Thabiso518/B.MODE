Maburu Tech Solutions - Business Model Canvas

On-campus laptop services and sales for students in Lesotho

What are you selling?
  • On-campus laptop services (repairs, software installation)
  • New and refurbished laptop sales
  • CampusTech mobile and web application
  • 3-month pay-later plans for students
Target Market
  • Primary: First-year university students
  • Secondary: Other students, lecturers, staff, community members
  • TAM: 25,000-30,000 tertiary students in Lesotho
  • SOM Year 1: 3,000 students (Limkokwing University)
Launch Strategy
  • Pilot at Limkokwing University
  • CampusTech app rollout
  • Social media and campus awareness campaigns
  • Partnerships with university IT departments
  • Discount incentives for early adopters
Post-Launch (First 30, 60 & 90 days)
  • 30 days: Gather feedback, fix issues, reach 100 bookings
  • 60 days: Introduce referrals, secure 2+ university partnerships
  • 90 days: 500+ active users, launch laptop sales, expand to NUL/Botho
Value Proposition
  • Convenient on-campus services
  • Student-affordable pricing
  • Flexible payment options
  • Trusted student-to-student service
  • App-based convenience
Distribution
  • CampusTech app (mobile and web)
  • Physical on-campus service points
  • Integrated digital payments (M-Pesa, EcoCash, cards)
  • Referral and loyalty programs
  • Partnerships with university IT departments
Competitors
  • Local off-campus repair shops (expensive, inconvenient)
  • Informal technicians (unreliable)
  • Other tech startups (limited student focus)
Who's buying it?
  • First-year students (priority)
  • Continuing students
  • Bursary-dependent students
  • Lecturers and university staff
  • Local community members near campuses
Metrics
User Growth
App downloads and active users
Service Volume
Bookings per week/month
Revenue
From services and laptop sales
Customer Satisfaction
App ratings and feedback
Retention
Repeat customers and referrals
Assumptions / Risks
  • Assumptions:
    • Students will prefer on-campus services over town options
    • Bursary disbursements will align with pay-later plans
    • Universities will support partnerships
  • Risks:
    • Delayed bursary payments affecting revenue
    • Technical issues with the app
    • Competition from established repair shops
    • Low adoption if students lack awareness
Considerations
  • Scalability: Plan for expansion to other universities and towns
  • Training: Employ student technicians to build local capacity
  • Sustainability: Explore partnerships with laptop suppliers for bulk discounts
  • Community impact: Create jobs for IT graduates and student interns
  • Technology: Ensure app reliability and continuous improvement

About Maburu Tech Solutions

Maburu is a student-focused technology support service based in Lesotho, providing on-campus laptop services, sales, and support through the CampusTech app. Designed by students for students, Maburu addresses the unique technology challenges faced by university students through affordable, convenient, and reliable solutions.
